\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We present Grassroots Logic Programs (GLP), a multiagent, concurrent, logic programming language designed for the implementation of smartphone-based, serverless, grassroots platforms---platforms that can operate independently and coalesce into ever larger instances. GLP extends Logic Programs with (1) reader/writer variable pairs, where each writer is paired with a reader that receives its value; (2) single-occurrence (SO): a variable may occur at most once in a goal or clause; and (3) single-reader single-writer (SRSW): a writer occurs in a clause iff its paired reader does. The operational semantics conjures both linear logic and futures/promises: an assignment may be produced at most once via a writer and consumed at most once via its paired reader. We define multiagent GLP (maGLP), which extends GLP with named agents communicating via reader assignments and cold-calls---a means for agents with no prior shared variables to establish communication. We prove that maGLP is grassroots. Programming is illustrated via a grassroots social graph.
\end{abstract}
